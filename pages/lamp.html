LAMP!: Designing ‘Moth-thew’

“LAMP!” is an endless obstacle-dodging Unity game that I made in a team. It features a moth, who dodges rainfall and other obstacles to reach his goal: the light of a lamp. We defined our target audience as young, casual players who like to explore and fantasise. The project task was to redesign a very rudimentary game within three iterations. I was the art lead and sole illustrator.

The problems with the original were endless: the game was coded with many errors, the gameplay was too repetitive and simple, and the graphics were unappealing. However, as the illustrator of the game, I was mostly focused on the graphics. In particular the design of the main enemy: the original enemy graphics featured realistic depictions of an antlion.

Our group found the antlion unattractive and inappropriate for most audiences. We tried ideating to make the antlion cuter. Taking info from characters in The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild, our character would have simpler shapes and more curves instead of sharp edges.

When we decided to code an endless obstacle-dodging game, the team felt that an antlion was too obscure and wanted to change our main character to be a more recognizable insect.

At the time, Vancouver was experiencing a whole horde of moths. We decided to be topical and make our character a moth. This is how ‘Moth-thew’ came to be. In this stage, the moth was a tad too simple. But I got what I was looking for, the colour scheme and general shape was well-received. I was told to add a pattern, so I did and sent it to the team for feedback.

For this final design, I brought back the use of rounded shapes to make the character shape language as clearly friendly as possible: most notably, I rounded out the body fur. The pointed antennae are to give the character a more distinct silhouette. Finally the pattern was used to break up the monotony of a singular colour and differentiate Moth-thew from being perceived as a butterfly. This was the design used for the final.

Shape language and silhouette are two of the most pivotal parts in character design. By keeping these concepts in mind, I created a friendly-looking insect with a cheerful colour palette that contrasts against the blue background of our game.
